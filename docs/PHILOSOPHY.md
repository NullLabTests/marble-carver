# Philosophy

## The Michelangelo Approach

> *Non ha l'ottimo artista alcun concetto*
> *ch'un marmo solo in sé non circoscriva*
> *col suo soverchio; e solo a quello arriva*
> *la man che ubbidisce all'intelletto.*
>
> — Michelangelo, Sonnet 151 (c. 1538–1544)

> "The best artist has no concept that a single marble block does not contain
> within itself — its excess removed; only the hand that obeys the intellect
> reaches that truth."

Michelangelo claimed he did not *invent* David or the Pietà. He saw them
already in the marble, and his genius was knowing exactly which stone to
remove.

---

## Research is subtractive

The dominant metaphor for research is **constructive**: we build theories,
stack experiments, assemble proofs. This has a hidden cost: it treats wrong
turns as waste, dead ends as shameful, and retractions as failures.

The carving metaphor flips this.

- The truth is already in the problem. It is objective, waiting, invariant.
- Every false hypothesis you test and discard is *progress*. You are removing
  something that isn't the truth.
- Every failed experiment narrows the space of what remains.
- Every retraction or correction is a victory — you found a piece that didn't
  belong, and you removed it.

---

## Visible failures

> "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work."
> — widely attributed to Edison

In the carving framework, a failure is not an embarrassment. It is a
**signpost** for everyone who follows. When you document a dead end, you save
future researchers — including your future self — from walking that path again.

That's why every attempt is numbered, dated, and preserved. That's why
original claims are never deleted, only annotated with audit notes.

---

## Retractions are strength

Conventional science treats retractions as stains. In the carving framework, a
retraction is a **clean cut**: you identified a piece of the marble that didn't
belong, and you removed it cleanly. The original remains visible so the cut can
be inspected by anyone.

The strength of a carving is visible in its corrections. A project with many
audit notes is not a project with many mistakes — it is a project that was
ruthless about removing what wasn't true.

---

## Mixing formal and empirical

Lean 4 proofs, mechanistic models, statistical analyses, and philosophical
arguments all belong in the same workshop. The carving framework doesn't
care what tool you use to remove marble — only that you remove it cleanly,
transparently, and in a way others can verify.

---

## From solo to swarm

A single researcher can use these templates with nothing but a text editor and
Git. The same templates work for a human+AI pair, a lab group, or a
multi-agent system where AI agents propose attempts, write audit notes, and
cross-reference citations.

The structure is the same. The discipline is the same. The only difference is
throughput.

---

## The goal

When you are done, nothing remains except the truth.

The marble is gone. The angel stands free.
